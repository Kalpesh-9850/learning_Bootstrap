Welcome to Pune

Pune is often called the “Oxford of the East” and the “Cultural Capital of Maharashtra.” The city is known for its history, educational institutes, IT hub, and most importantly, its deep cultural and religious roots. One of the most iconic landmarks of Pune is the Shreemant Dagadusheth Halwai Ganpati Temple. Established in the late 19th century, this temple is a symbol of devotion and philanthropy. The majestic Ganpati idol, nearly 7.5 feet tall, is adorned with gold ornaments and is worshipped by lakhs of devotees every year. The temple is especially famous during the Ganesh Chaturthi festival, when devotees from across India come to seek blessings. Another very important and historic Ganapati in Pune is the Guruji Talim Ganapati. Established in 1887, it is one of the oldest Sarvajanik Ganesh Mandals in Pune. It is also called “Manacha Pahila Ganpati” (मानाचा पहिला गणपती) — meaning the first respected Ganapati of Pune. The title “Manacha Ganapati” represents honor and prestige during the Ganeshotsav festival. Every year, Guruji Talim Ganapati leads the immersion procession in Pune, signifying its cultural importance.
